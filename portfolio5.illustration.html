PHISH MAYAN REDESIGN
The hieroglyphs of the ancient Maya is one of the worlds most beautiful forms of writing, however, stone carvings often yielded sloppy lines and hard-to-read glyps. I thought it deserved a makeover. My goal for this project was to design a Mexico themed screen-printed concert poster for the fans the Rock and Roll superstars Phish. I wanted to capture the beauty of the ancient Maya writings, while invoking a feeling of modernity, elegance, and relaxation.
GETTING STARTED

My first step is demographic analysis. From a poll of 2,438 Phish Fans or "Phans," I learned that 44.5% are 25-34 years old, and 27.2% are 35-44 years old. I also learned that over 80% of Phans are male, and over 50% have seen at least 31 total shows.

I gathered that my target audience would be the young professional male with an extreme emotional connection and loyalty to the band. They are likely very familiar with Phish lyrics and themes. While the typical Phan is likely inspired by nostaligic printing methods like screen-printing, they have graduated from the classic "dorm-room" Rock and Roll art to a more mature and classier "family living-room" style.

MAYA GRAPHIC RESEARCH

Next, I researched Maya glyphs, architecture, art, and culture. I also researched todays interpretations of Maya style art. I discovered that the Maya Calendar and "Hunab Ku," were drastically over-represented. My goal was to find graphics that were recognizably mayan, without cliche overused themes. It was important for me to create a unique rendering.

CLEAN IT UP

I transformed a collection of graphics with familiar Maya themes and unique content to invoke a feeling of modernity and elegance. I performed this by redrawing the images using graph and tracing-paper, utilizing clean 90 degree angles.

CLEAN IT UP MORE

I then scanned and patiently retraced the images using Adobe illustrator. Many of the redrawn graphics looked too modern, so I created a border radius on the 90 degree angles to find the perfect balance of traditional Maya and unique modern style.
